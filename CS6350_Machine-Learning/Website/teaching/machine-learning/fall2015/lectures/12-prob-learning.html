Probabilistic Learning

←Previous lecture  |  Home  |  Next lecture →

Starting this lecture we will look at probabilistic criteria for learning. First, we will see maximum a posteriori and maximum likelihood learning criteria with examples. Then we will look at the very popular naive Bayes classifier and its decision boundary.

This will take us on a discussion about generative and discriminative models. We will end this section by looking at logistic regression.
